Lessons Learned

WannaCry provided several key lessons for organizations worldwide. First, keeping systems updated with the latest security patches is essential. Many infections occurred because organizations were running outdated Windows versions without critical updates.

Second, proper network segmentation and isolation can prevent malware from spreading uncontrollably within an organization. The flat network structures in hospitals and enterprises allowed WannaCry to move laterally with ease.

Third, maintaining reliable backups and tested disaster recovery plans is vital. Even if files are encrypted, backups allow organizations to restore operations without paying ransom.

Finally, WannaCry highlighted the need for cybersecurity awareness and training, particularly for critical sectors like healthcare. Employees should recognize suspicious activity, phishing attempts, and the risks of unpatched systems.
