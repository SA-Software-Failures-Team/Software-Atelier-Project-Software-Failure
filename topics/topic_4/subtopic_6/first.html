Introduction


WannaCry was a fast-spreading ransomware attack that erupted in May 2017, disrupting hospitals, businesses, and public services across the globe. It exploited a critical Windows vulnerability called EternalBlue, which allowed it to spread automatically through network connections.

Within hours, WannaCry infected more than 230,000 computers in over 150 countries. It encrypted users’ files and demanded payment in Bitcoin to restore access. The attack caused severe disruption, especially to the UK’s National Health Service (NHS), where thousands of appointments and operations were cancelled and medical services were temporarily halted.

The WannaCry outbreak revealed how outdated and unpatched systems can turn a single vulnerability into a worldwide crisis. It emphasized the importance of timely security updates, network segmentation, and well-tested backup and recovery procedures.
