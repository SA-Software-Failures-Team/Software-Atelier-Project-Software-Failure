Root Cause & Technical Analysis


The primary cause of the exposure was misconfiguration of PACS servers. Many systems were left reachable from the public internet without any authentication enforced. The DICOM protocol — widely used to transfer and store medical images — was accessible in cleartext and allowed direct retrieval of image files and associated metadata.

In addition to open network access, many installations lacked basic protections such as TLS encryption, firewall rules, and strong access controls. Some servers used default credentials or outdated software that had never been patched, further increasing risk.

In short, this was not primarily a software bug but an operational failure: a combination of poor configuration, insufficient network hygiene, and weak governance over medical IT systems left sensitive data exposed.
