Business-Critical Systems
Bugs in business-critical systems can have severe consequences because these systems often manage essential operations like financial transactions, healthcare processes, or transportation control. Even a small error can lead to massive financial losses, service outages, or safety hazards. For example, a rounding bug in a banking system might miscalculate interest across millions of accounts, while a software glitch in an airline reservation system could disrupt global travel. These failures are costly not only in terms of money but also reputation and compliance, as many industries are heavily regulated. To mitigate risks, organizations rely on rigorous testing, formal verification for critical components, redundancy, and continuous monitoring.
