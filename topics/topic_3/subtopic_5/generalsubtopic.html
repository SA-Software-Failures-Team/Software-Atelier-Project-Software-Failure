Knight Capital Group (USA, 2012)

The Knight Capital Group incident (USA, 2012) was a catastrophic algorithmic trading failure where a bug in a newly deployed software update accidentally activated dormant, defective code on one server, causing a runaway trade loop that executed millions of unwanted orders in 45 minutes, resulting in a $440 million loss and immediate financial collapse for the Group.
