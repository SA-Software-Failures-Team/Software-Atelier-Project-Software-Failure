Heartbleed - OpenSSL, 2014

A severe vulnerability identified as CVE-2014-0160 was publicly revealed in the OpenSSL library on April 7, 2014. OpenSSL is a cryptographic library that secures internet traffic for websites, email servers, VPNs, and many other systems. The bug, known as Heartbleed, was caused by a coding flaw in the TLS Heartbeat extension, which let attackers extract up to 64 KB of data from the memory of vulnerable servers or clients, all without needing to authenticate.
