Impact & Scope of the Outage


The outage had a global footprint. Users in Europe, Asia, and the Americas reported issues connecting to Teams meetings, sending mails via Outlook and Exchange Online, accessing SharePoint and OneDrive, and reaching applications hosted on Azure. For many organisations, collaboration and internal communication were partially or completely interrupted for part of the morning.

Because the root problem sat in Microsoft’s WAN, the impact was horizontal: it affected multiple services at once instead of a single isolated product. This demonstrated how tightly integrated Microsoft’s cloud environment is — and how a fault in shared network infrastructure becomes a single point of failure for thousands of customers.

The outage also highlighted dependency chains. Governments, schools, hospitals, and companies relying on Microsoft for identity, messaging, file storage and cloud hosting suddenly discovered that a change they did not control could temporarily pause key operations. Even a 60–90 minute disruption at this scale has measurable economic and organisational effects.


  • Global Reach

    • Users worldwide lost access to core Microsoft 365 services.

  • Shared Backbone

    • A single WAN issue disrupted many services in parallel.

  • Critical Dependence

    • Public and private sectors depend on few large cloud providers.
