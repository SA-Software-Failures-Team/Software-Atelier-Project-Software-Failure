Deepwater Horizon Oil Rig Incident (2010)

The Deepwater Horizon explosion and oil spill in April 2010 revealed how modern offshore drilling is tightly connected to software, sensors, and automated safety systems. A mixture of technical failures, misconfigured alarms, and misread data turned a single well control problem into one of the largest environmental disasters in history. This subtopic explores what happened, the role of software and control systems, how human decisions interacted with technology, and what this means for energy infrastructure.
