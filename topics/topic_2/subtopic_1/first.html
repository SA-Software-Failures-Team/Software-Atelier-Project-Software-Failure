What Was the Denver Automated Baggage System?

Denver International Airport (DIA) wanted a single, integrated, fully automated baggage system for all airlines and terminals. Instead of traditional conveyors and manual tugs, bags would travel in small, radio-controlled carts on miles of track, guided by scanners, sensors and central control software.

The design included:
– Tens of thousands of meters of conveyors and track
– Thousands of intelligent carts
– Bar-code scanners, electric eyes and sensors to track each bag
– Networked computers coordinating routes and timing

The goal was to create a high-speed, high-tech logistics infrastructure inside the airport: shorter connection times, fewer lost bags, and a “smart” airport image.

On paper, it was a showcase of advanced software, automation and infrastructure engineering. In practice, the level of complexity, tight deadlines and constant design changes made it extremely fragile.
