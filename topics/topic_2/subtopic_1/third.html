Software, Design & Management Issues


Software Complexity
The control software had to coordinate thousands of carts, switches and scanners in real time. The system was highly coupled: a small timing bug could break an entire route. Full-scale integration tests were limited, so many defects appeared only when the whole airport was connected. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Changing Requirements
Airlines and airport management requested changes even after design started: new destinations for bags, modified layouts, different operating rules. Instead of adjusting scope and schedule, changes were forced into an already fragile design, increasing risk.

Lack of Realistic Testing & Governance
The project ran on fixed deadlines and political pressure. There was little time for gradual rollout, simulation or proper fallback strategies. Responsibilities between city, contractors and airlines were blurred. This combination of technical debt and weak management turned a promising idea into a famous failure.


  • Software Complexity

    • Unproven large-scale control software with tight timing and many dependencies.

  • Scope Changes

    • Late requirement changes without adjusting time or architecture.

  • Weak Governance

    • No phased rollout, limited testing, unclear ownership of risks.
