Impact on the Airport & Lessons for Digital Infrastructure

Delays & Costs
Problems with the automated baggage system were a main reason DIA’s opening was delayed by many months, costing the city hundreds of millions of dollars to keep an empty airport ready and to redesign backup systems. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}


Reputation & Partial Abandonment
The system became a global example of “high-tech gone wrong”. In the end, only a reduced subset was used for a single concourse; most baggage handling switched to more traditional, simpler solutions. The original vision of a fully automated, integrated system was never achieved.


Key Lessons for Critical Software Projects
– Do not scale unproven technology to “heroic” size in one step.
– Use phased deployment and realistic testing on the real infrastructure.
– Keep scope, schedule and budget flexible when requirements change.
– Ensure clear responsibility for system engineering and risk management.


The Denver baggage system failure shows that digital infrastructure is infrastructure: when core services (like baggage handling in a hub airport) depend on complex software, weak design and bad coordination can disrupt an entire transport system. It is a warning for any modern smart airport, smart port or smart grid project.
