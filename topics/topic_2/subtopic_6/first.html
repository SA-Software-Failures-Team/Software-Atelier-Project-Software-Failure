What is Stuxnet?

Stuxnet is a highly sophisticated worm that specifically targeted Siemens Step7/WinCC industrial control systems. Its objective was to modify PLC logic to cause subtle physical damage to centrifuge systems at Iran’s Natanz nuclear facility.


High-Level Architecture Propagation and persistence mechanisms Command-and-control communication over HTTP Industrial payload injected into PLC logic


Timeline (2009–2011) 2009: Earliest Stuxnet variants likely deployed. June 2010: Malware discovered after unusual behavior on industrial systems September 2010: Technical analysis released by Symantec. 2011: Full dossier published documenting its architecture and spread.

Characteristics Highly targeted – designed for a single industrial environment. First known malware to cause physical sabotage via PLCs. Used multiple zero-day vulnerabilities and stolen digital certificates.
