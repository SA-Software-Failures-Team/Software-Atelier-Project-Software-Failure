Jeep Cherokee Uconnect Vulnerability (2015)

In 2015, security researchers Charlie Miller and Chris Valasek demonstrated a critical, zero-day vulnerability in the Uconnect infotainment system used by Fiat Chrysler. They proved it was possible to remotely hack a 2014 Jeep Cherokee over the cellular network, gaining control of the radio, wipers, and—most catastrophically—the vehicle's steering, transmission, and brakes. This demonstration exposed the life-threatening dangers of software vulnerabilities in connected vehicles, forcing a recall of 1.4 million vehicles.
