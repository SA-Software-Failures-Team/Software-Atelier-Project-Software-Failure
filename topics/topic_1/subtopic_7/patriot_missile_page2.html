Patriot Missile System - Technical Analysis


The Patriot system tracked time in tenths of a second using a 24-bit fixed-point register. The radar miscalculated the position of an approaching missile by more than 500 meters after 100 hours of continuous operation due to a minor rounding error.

Timeline of the Failure:

  • Day 0: The Patriot system began to run constantly in Dhahran.
  • Hour 100: Radar got imprecise due to cumulative rounding mistake.
  • February 25, 1991: Incoming Scud missile not intercepted.
  • Immediate Result: 28 killed, 100+ injured.
  • Aftermath: A software patch was used to regularly reset the timer.

Lessons Learned / Prevention:
- Always take long-term accuracy into account while developing essential software.
- Incorporate system resets on a regular basis to prevent accumulating mistakes.
- Extensive testing in real-world operational environments is essential.
- Even small rounding errors can have catastrophic consequences.
