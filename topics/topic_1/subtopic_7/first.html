Patriot Missile System Software Failure

Author: Juliana Silva Costa

Background

Incoming missiles can be detected, tracked, and intercepted by the Patriot Missile Defense System. During the Gulf War, it was widely employed. But in 1991, a catastrophic breakdown resulted from a significant software vulnerability.

The Failure

On February 25, 1991, an Iraqi Scud missile attacked a U.S. Army barracks in Dhahran, Saudi Arabia, killing 28 troops and wounded around 100 others when a Patriot missile battery failed to stop it. A software bug in the internal clock of the machine was the reason.

Cause of the Vulnerability

The system measured time in decimals from startup. After nearly 100 hours of continuous operation, a rounding error appeared in the calculation of time, causing the radar to miscalculate the missile's position by more than half a kilometer. As a result, the system completely lost track of the incoming missile.
