Introduction: The Software-Defined Warplane

This research explores the F-35 Lightning II as a primary case study in modern project software failure. More than any aircraft in history, the F-35's capabilities are defined by its code. This analysis is divided into three parts: the catastrophic failure of its ALIS logistics network, the critical bugs found in its Operational Flight Program (OFP), and the crushing financial and strategic consequences of these software-driven failures.
