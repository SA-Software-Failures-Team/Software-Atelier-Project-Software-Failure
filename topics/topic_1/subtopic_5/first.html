Background

The F22 Raptor is an advanced stealth fighter aircraft. During early operational testing, a software bug in its flight control system was discovered, which could potentially cause control issues in certain edge-case scenarios.

The Bug

The bug involved the flight control algorithms misinterpreting sensor data under specific conditions. It did not cause any known crashes, but it required immediate correction to ensure safe operation.

Impact and Prevention

The bug led to a temporary suspension of some test flights. Developers patched the software, added more rigorous simulation checks, and implemented long-term monitoring to prevent recurrence.
