The Ariane 5 Disaster: A Very Expensive Software Bug

The maiden flight of the European Space Agency's (ESA) Ariane 5 rocket, Flight 501, ended in catastrophic failure on June 4, 1996, just 37 seconds after lift-off. The explosion, which destroyed the rocket and its four scientific satellites, is widely considered the most expensive single computer bug in history.
