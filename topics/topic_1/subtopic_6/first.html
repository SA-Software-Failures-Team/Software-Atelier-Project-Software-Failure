What Happened: The Proximate Cause


The crisis centered on two accidents: Lion Air Flight 610 on October 29, 2018, and Ethiopian Airlines Flight 302 on March 10, 2019, which together claimed the lives of 346 people.



In both instances, shortly after takeoff, an erroneous input from a single Angle of Attack (AoA) sensor triggered the MCAS. The AoA sensor measures the angle between the wing's chord line and the relative wind. The MCAS, designed to automatically push the aircraftâ€™s nose down to prevent an aerodynamic stall in high-angle-of-attack situations, repeatedly commanded the horizontal stabilizer to pitch the nose down, despite the pilots' efforts to counteract the movement.



The flight crews, unaware of the powerful, repetitive nature of the MCAS and lacking the necessary training or documentation to fully understand and disable the system under extreme stress, were unable to regain control. The aircraft ultimately entered a catastrophic dive, crashing minutes after takeoff.
