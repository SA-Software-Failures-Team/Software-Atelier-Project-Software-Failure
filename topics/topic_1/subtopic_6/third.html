Impact & Scope: Global Grounding and Financial Devastation

346 lives were lost, creating an unimaginable tragedy for families and a profound blow to public trust in air travel safety.


Following the second crash, regulators worldwide, starting with China and later the FAA, grounded the entire 737 MAX fleet (387 aircraft in service at the time) from March 2019. This unprecedented grounding lasted for 20 months in the US, crippling airline operations globally.


Boeing faced an estimated $20 billion in direct costs, including compensation to airlines, fines, and legal fees. Total losses, including cancelled orders and indirect effects, exceeded $60 billion.
